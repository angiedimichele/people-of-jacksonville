Jacksonville Stories

The city of Jacksonville is one of the largest in the country. It is home to diverse people with unique stories.

The streets, buildings and bus stations can each give way to interesting stories if you lend an ear.

These are stories of five people in Jacksonville who persevered in the face of adversity and now work tirelessly to be a beacon of light for others in their city.

The following stories are of Bethany Baker, a deaf nurse; Valentino Dixon, a former wrongfully convicted felon; Alexus Torney, a 9-year-old with medical conditions that do not allow her to speak; Marlena Roberts, a homeless mother; and Latasha Hobbs, a leader in her local Moms Demand Action group.
